Today

What in Twain's descriptions reads as a plague that can be solved as quickly as possible, we now regard as salvation!
140 years after the first encounter, we live in a present where work has not become less by technical progress, but where it is precisely technology that makes the work so omnipresent.
The eroding of the boundaries between work and leisure, the internalized neoliberal paradigms of constant availability, endless progress, lifelong learning, and unbridled creativity, we now recognize - are not infinite jest, but never-ending work. Where the knowledge of this dictum of work is lacking, or the fear of being left behind in order not to lose a market advantage too great, or where one's own strength is insufficient to break the social conditioning, there lies the parasitic potential of vermis auricularis. It has the ability to find hosts where needed the most - where people can not find an end to their labor, where the thoughts of productivity reach into their sleep. There, the vermis auricularis can eat itself fat.
