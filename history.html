History

One of the earliest, at least written, encounters with vermis auricularis goes back to Mark Twain. Already in 1876 he held his own infestation, without naming the species, but having brought to paper a detailed recollection of his encounter. According to his lively description all actions such as reading, sleeping, conversation or forming simple thoughts were rendered impossible:
“I fought hard for an hour, but it was useless. My head kept humming, [...] and so on and so on, without peace or respite. The day's work was ruined.” He finally could expel the parasite, only through passing it on to a dear friend. After also being tormented for days, locked in limbo of the forced "low attention state", his friend and follow up host sought help from Mark Twain, who came up with a solution: “How did I finally save him from an asylum? I took him to a neighboring university and made him discharge the burden of his persecuting rhymes into the eager ears of the poor, unthinking students.”
